About Hieronymus Bosch

Hieronymus Bosch was a Dutch painter and draftsman active in the late 15th and early 16th centuries. He is known for his highly imaginative and fantastical works, many of which depict religious themes and moral lessons. Bosch's paintings are characterized by their use of symbolism and their intricate, often surreal imagery.

One of Bosch's most famous works is The Garden of Earthly Delights, a triptych painting that depicts the creation of the world, the pleasures of the earthly life, and the torments of the damned in Hell. The painting is renowned for its detailed and imaginative depiction of both heaven and hell, as well as for its use of symbolism to convey moral and religious themes. Bosch's works continue to be widely studied and admired for their originality and artistic merit

Bosch's influence can be seen in the works of many later artists, particularly those of the Surrealist movement in the 20th century.
